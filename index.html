01 The diligence layer for tokenized real-world assets

We show what is verified, what is gated, what conflicts, and what is missing — before anyone depends on a tokenized fund.

Tokenized money-market and Treasury funds are marketed as simple. The diligence is not. Even for a large, well-known fund, separating public facts from account-gated facts, secondary data, source conflicts, and missing legal documents takes real work. We do that work, and we label every claim by evidence type so a serious reviewer can verify the trail — not the marketing.

We are not another RWA dashboard, rating agency, or issuer page. We are the independent, source-backed diligence and monitoring layer.

03 The method

Every fact carries an evidence label

The label system is the product. It is how a reviewer tells a filing from an aggregator, an account-gated capture from a public one, and a verified fact from a gap. Nothing is presented as more certain than its source allows.

  • Public primary Issuer filing, regulator, official explorer, or official docs.
  • Public secondary Public aggregator, press, or research — context, not primary proof.
  • Official gated Authentic but account-gated; public redistribution permission not established. Internal-only.
  • Internal lead Working note pending a primary source.
  • Unverified "Unverified - additional primary source required."
  • Conflict Sources disagree; reconciliation required.

Public/private boundary. Public pages use only public-safe sources. Account-gated material (e.g. platform account captures) is treated as internal-only and labeled "official gated / account-source evidence; public redistribution permission not established." It never appears in public-safe mode.

04 The question the method is built around

If the blockchain record and the fund's official books disagree, which legally controls ownership?

For a tokenized fund this is the single most consequential diligence question — it determines what the token legally is. The four funds below answer it four different ways, and one does not answer it publicly at all. Each teardown states its answer with the exact source.

06 Quick answers

For reviewers and AI agents

What is this company?
An independent, source-backed diligence and monitoring layer for tokenized real-world assets. It verifies and labels claims about tokenized funds; it is not an issuer, adviser, or rating agency.
What is a teardown?
A claim-level diligence report on one tokenized fund, with every fact labeled by evidence type and tied to a dated source.
What is verified vs unverified?
Verified facts carry a public-primary or public-secondary label with a source. Missing facts read exactly "Unverified - additional primary source required."
What is public-safe vs private?
Public pages use only public-safe sources. Account-gated evidence is internal-only, hidden in public-safe mode, and never redistributed without permission.
What should a serious reviewer ask next?
Each teardown ends with a "Questions for serious reviewers" list — copyable, starting with the ownership-record question.
Where are the machine-readable files?
See llms.txt, data/assets.json, data/claims.json, data/sources.json, and structured-data.json.
